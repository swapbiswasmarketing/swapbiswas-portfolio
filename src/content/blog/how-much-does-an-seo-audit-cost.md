**[Ahrefs surveyed 439 SEO professionals](https://ahrefs.com/blog/seo-pricing/) and found that the most common per-project fee charged by SEO agencies falls between $2,501 and $5,000** - yet the full range in the same survey stretches from under $500 to well over $10,000 for a single engagement. That is the problem with **how much an SEO audit cost** figures circulate online: the "average" is hiding a 10x spread, and most buyers have no way to tell which end of the range they should actually be paying.

This post breaks down the real cost of an SEO audit in 2026, what drives the price up or down, and how to tell a serious audit from a templated PDF. Every number below comes from published pricing pages or industry surveys, not guesses.

## How Much Does an SEO Audit Cost? The 2026 Price Ranges

There is no single answer because "SEO audit" covers everything from a one-hour sanity check to a 60-page technical and content teardown. The industry breaks down into three pricing tiers that line up with website size and audit depth.

| Audit Tier | Typical Price | Best For | Scope |
|------------|--------------|----------|-------|
| **Basic** | $300 - $800 | Small sites, startups, <50 pages | Technical health check, top 10 issues |
| **Comprehensive** | $1,500 - $5,000 | Mid-market, 50-5,000 pages, competitive niches | Technical + on-page + content + backlinks + competitive gap |
| **Enterprise** | $10,000+ | Large sites, e-commerce, multi-region | Full stack audit, log files, international SEO, migration prep |

These ranges are consistent with [Kleverish's 2025 SEO audit pricing breakdown](https://www.kleverish.com/blog/2025-seo-audit-pricing-explained/), which cites $300 to $800 for basic audits, $1,500 to $5,000 for comprehensive audits, and $10,000 and up for enterprise work.

For context on where audits sit within the broader SEO pricing landscape: according to [Ahrefs' SEO pricing survey](https://ahrefs.com/blog/seo-pricing/), which polled 439 SEO professionals, the most common per-project fee for SEO agencies falls in the $2,501 to $5,000 range. Agencies also charge roughly double the monthly retainer of freelancers.

## What Actually Drives the Price of an SEO Audit

The same website can get quoted $800 or $8,000 depending on what the auditor is being asked to produce. These six factors explain most of the variance.

### 1. Site Size and Technical Complexity

A 40-page brochure site takes a few hours to crawl and assess. A 40,000-page e-commerce site with faceted navigation, multiple subdomains, and product variants needs log file analysis, crawl budget modeling, and indexation strategy - all of which take days, not hours.

As a rough rule: every 10x increase in page count adds a tier of complexity, and therefore cost.

### 2. Depth of the Analysis

A basic audit answers "what is broken?" A comprehensive audit answers "what is broken, why is it broken, how do we fix it, and in what order?" The deeper the causal analysis, the more senior the analyst, the higher the rate.

If you want a prioritized roadmap rather than a list of issues, expect to pay comprehensive-tier pricing.

### 3. Industry Competition

Auditing an SEO strategy in a low-competition niche (local service businesses, B2B SaaS in an emerging category) is simpler than auditing one in law, finance, or insurance - where every keyword has 20 competing sites with million-dollar link budgets. More competitive industries require deeper backlink analysis, content gap research, and SERP feature tracking.

### 4. Deliverable Format

A written report is one thing. A report plus a 90-minute walkthrough call, a prioritized Jira backlog, and follow-up Q&A is another. Every extra deliverable adds hours.

### 5. Tools Used

Professional SEO audits lean on a stack of paid tools. Running one audit does not justify annual subscriptions, but the cost is baked into the price you pay. As published on their pricing pages, representative tool costs include:

- **[Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/pricing) Lite**: $129/month
- **Semrush Pro**: $139.95/month (see [Semrush pricing](https://www.semrush.com/pricing/))
- **[Screaming Frog SEO Spider](https://www.screamingfrog.co.uk/seo-spider/licence/)**: £199/year per user license

### 6. Who Is Doing the Work

The same audit costs very different amounts depending on who runs it. An in-house analyst charges nothing extra, while a mid-level freelancer or consultant typically bills in the $75 to $150 per hour range consistent with the most common rates reported in the [Ahrefs SEO pricing survey](https://ahrefs.com/blog/seo-pricing/).

A senior consultant or boutique agency can bill $200 or more per hour, and large agencies often price projects at a flat fee that reflects senior oversight, project management, and brand premium.

## Freelancer vs Agency vs DIY: Which Audit Is Right for You?

Pricing is only half the decision. The other half is matching the audit type to your situation.

### Freelancer ($500 - $3,000)

Best for small and mid-market sites that need one experienced pair of eyes rather than a full team. You get direct access to the person doing the work, faster turnaround, and lower overhead. The trade-off: depth varies wildly by individual, and you are trusting one person's opinion without a second reviewer.

Use a freelancer when you have a clear scope, a tight budget, and you can vet the individual's past work.

### Agency ($2,500 - $15,000)

Best when you need a team with multiple specialists: a technical SEO lead, a content strategist, a backlink analyst, sometimes a developer. Agencies typically follow a repeatable audit methodology, which means more consistent quality and cleaner deliverables. You are also paying for project management, QA, and a polished final report.

Use an agency when the audit will inform a multi-quarter SEO strategy, or when you need buy-in from stakeholders who will trust a named firm more than a solo operator.

### DIY (Free - $200 in tool costs)

Best for small sites and early-stage founders who need to understand their SEO problems without writing a check. You can cover most technical and on-page issues using:

- **[Google Search Console](https://search.google.com/search-console/about)** - free, catches indexation, core web vitals, and basic performance issues
- **[Google PageSpeed Insights](https://pagespeed.web.dev/)** - free, measures Core Web Vitals per page
- **Screaming Frog SEO Spider (free tier)** - crawls up to 500 URLs at no cost

The limitation: you will not get prioritization, competitive benchmarks, or a second opinion on your assumptions. For a walkthrough of what to actually check, see our guide on [how to conduct a technical SEO site audit](/blog/how-to-conduct-a-technical-seo-site-audit/).

## What Should Be Included in a Comprehensive SEO Audit

Before you approve any audit proposal, make sure the scope covers these areas. If a $3,000 audit skips half of them, you are overpaying.

### Technical SEO
- Crawlability and indexation (robots.txt, XML sitemaps, canonical tags, noindex rules)
- Site architecture and internal linking depth
- Page speed and Core Web Vitals (LCP, INP, CLS)
- Mobile usability
- Structured data and schema validation
- HTTPS, redirects, and broken links

### On-Page SEO
- Title tags, meta descriptions, and H1 coverage
- Content quality scoring on top pages
- Keyword cannibalization
- Thin or duplicate content flags
- Image optimization

### Content and Topical Coverage
- Content gap analysis vs. top 3 competitors
- Underperforming pages and their root causes
- Topic cluster mapping
- Outdated content that needs refreshing

### Backlinks and Authority
- Referring domain quality and growth trend
- Toxic link audit
- Competitor link gap analysis
- Lost links recovery opportunities

### Competitive Landscape
- Share of voice for priority keywords
- SERP feature capture rates
- Competitor publishing velocity

If your audit proposal only covers technical SEO, it is a **technical audit**, not a full SEO audit. Pay accordingly.

## Red Flags in SEO Audit Pricing

These are signs you are being overcharged or under-served.

- **Flat "$99 SEO audit" offers.** These are lead magnets for link-building services, not real audits. The deliverable is usually a generic Sitebulb or SEMrush export with minimal analysis.
- **No named deliverables in the proposal.** A legitimate audit contract specifies the exact sections, page count, and review format. Vague language means vague work.
- **Prices based on your revenue rather than your site size.** An audit costs what it costs to execute. Revenue-based pricing is a red flag for value-extraction billing.
- **Promises of specific ranking improvements in the audit itself.** An audit diagnoses. It does not guarantee outcomes. Any auditor who promises position changes before they have looked at the site is selling you something else.
- **No access to the analyst doing the work.** If the agency will not let you speak to the actual auditor before signing, the person on the sales call is not the person who will deliver the report.

## How to Calculate the ROI of an SEO Audit

An audit is worth paying for when it unlocks revenue you would not have captured otherwise. The math is simple:

**ROI = (Traffic value recovered + New opportunities identified) - Audit cost**

A single indexation bug that was blocking 20% of your product pages from ranking can return many times the cost of a $5,000 audit in the first quarter after the fix. A recommendation to consolidate three cannibalizing blog posts that moves a page from page 2 into the top 3 is usually the highest ROI recommendation in a typical audit, since click-through rates on page 2 are a small fraction of page 1. According to [Backlinko's analysis of 4 million Google search results](https://backlinko.com/google-ctr-stats), the #1 organic result receives around a 27.6% CTR - roughly 10 times more than position 10.

The worst-case ROI of an audit is not "it didn't improve traffic." It is "the recommendations were never implemented."

Before you pay for an audit, make sure you or your team have the bandwidth and technical ability to act on the recommendations. Otherwise, you are paying for a PDF.

## The Bottom Line: How Much Does an SEO Audit Cost?

- **Basic audits ($300 - $800)** make sense for small sites, startups, and founders who need a health check, not a strategic roadmap.
- **Comprehensive audits ($1,500 - $5,000)** are the sweet spot for most mid-market businesses - deep enough to drive a real action plan, priced reasonably for the work involved.
- **Enterprise audits ($10,000+)** are justified for large e-commerce sites, multi-region businesses, and post-migration diagnostics where the cost of getting it wrong dwarfs the audit fee.

So how much does an SEO audit cost in practice? Whatever you are willing to pay for recommendations you will actually act on. A $500 audit acted on beats a $5,000 audit filed away every single time.

Before you buy, confirm three things: scope is documented, the analyst is accessible, and your team has the bandwidth to implement the fixes.
